Top Destinations to Explore in Kerala During the Monsoon

Alappuzha (Alleppey)

Known as the 'Venice of the East', Alappuzha is famous for its scenic backwaters, houseboats, and lush paddy fields.

Kovalam

A serene beach town near Thiruvananthapuram, Kovalam is perfect for sunbathing, ayurvedic massages, and seaside stays.

Munnar

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Munnar is a paradise for tea lovers and nature enthusiasts, offering misty hills and waterfalls.

Kumarakom

A tranquil village on Vembanad Lake, Kumarakom offers beautiful houseboat cruises and rich birdlife in its sanctuary.

Kannur

Known as the “Land of Looms and Lore,” Kannur is a captivating blend of history, culture, and coastal charm. Famous for its ancient forts, pristine beaches, and vibrant Theyyam performances, Kannur offers a glimpse into Kerala’s living traditions

Kochi

Kochi, often called the “Queen of the Arabian Sea,” is a vibrant coastal city where tradition and modernity blend seamlessly. Once a bustling port in the spice trade, Kochi today is a cosmopolitan hub that reflects Kerala’s rich cultural mosaic.

Vagamon

Vagamon, often called Kerala’s hidden paradise, is a serene hill station draped in mist and greenery. Nestled in the Western Ghats, it offers rolling meadows, pine forests, and cascading waterfalls that create a dreamlike escape from city life.

Munroe Island , kerala

Munroe Island, a tranquil gem in Kerala’s backwaters, is where the Ashtamudi Lake meets a network of canals and lagoons. Known for its serene beauty, traditional coir-making villages.

Wayanadu,kerala

Wayanad, the emerald jewel of Kerala, is a lush hill district adorned with misty mountains, dense forests, and cascading waterfalls. Known for its rich wildlife, spice plantations, and ancient tribal culture

Poovar Island, Kerala

Poovar, a serene coastal village in Kerala, is where the Neyyar River meets the Arabian Sea
